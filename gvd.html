Guilded vs. Discord


Guilded vs. Discord / David vs. Goliath

When it comes to communicating with your friends, an easy to use app is always welcome. Nice if it is cheap, or even better, free. The two apps I will talk about here are pretty much "slack" lookalikes, but with a focus on everyone instead of being mainly business/office oriented. I will talk about Discord and Guilded, what they are and what the big differences are between them, and which one I prefer. You might not have heard of Guilded, but most likely you have heard of Discord, the popular app that is used by a variety of people.

Discord



Discord is a great app with lots and lots of cool and useful features, but it does lock a lot behind a paywall. If it was JUST cosmetics that are being locked behind a paywall, that would be fine, but sadly, decent audio/video/screenshare quality and decent filesharing size is locked behind their "Discord Nitro" subscription service.

Nitro (9,99€) offers you those things and cosmetic things like animated profile picture, animated emotes, using emotes globally (outside of the emotes origin Discord server) and more.

If you care about audio or video quality, it'll get a bit... pricey. If you want to have a Discord Server with good audio/video/screenshare quality (256kb/s a/v, 1080p60fps screenshare) which requires so called "boosts" you can apply to the server, then you'd have to pay 24,78€ for 7 boosts (3,54€ per boost with Nitro) ontop of Nitro, per month.

Guilded



Guilded is a competitor to Discord. Oh yes! maybe I will put the entire explanation here at some point why Guilded has so much potential, but in a nutshell, most features that discord wants money for, like good audio quality, is free on guilded. Plus, Guilded has a ton of built in things you don't have on discord, like calendar, scheduling, polls, etc.
